Capital Flow

Transparent money movement from funding to distribution.

Investor funds are wired to a licensed title company escrow account, capital is allocated to acquisition and rehab reserves, and net proceeds return to the LLC for final accounting before distributions are issued.

InvestorsCapital commitment is confirmed on a deal-by-deal basis.
Title EscrowFunds are wired to licensed third-party escrow, never to an individual.
LLC AcquisitionProperty is acquired under Ivory Oak Capital LLC or project-specific LLC.
RenovationRehab funds are controlled by title/lender holdback or project LLC account.
Exit & DistributionNet proceeds return to the LLC, capital is returned, then profits are split.
Investment Structure

Aligned structures for different investor profiles.

Final terms are defined prior to funding and can be structured around profit split, preferred return, or performance-based tiers.

Standard Split

65% / 35%

Investors receive 100% of initial capital back first. Remaining profit is split 65% to investors and 35% to Ivory Oak Capital.

Preferred Return

6%–8%

Investors receive capital back, then a preferred return, with remaining profit split according to the agreed structure.

Sliding Scale

70/30 → 60/40

Deal performance determines the split: lower-margin projects prioritize investors while stronger upside rewards execution.

Investor Protections

Structured to prioritize capital protection first.

Every deal is filtered for margin potential, controlled renovation scope, clear exit options, short-duration exposure, and transparent investor reporting.

Escrow Handling

Investor capital is wired through a licensed title company escrow account for third-party handling and transaction documentation.

Project Controls

Rehab budgets are tracked by line item with phased releases, progress photos, and separation from unrelated operating funds.

Reporting

Investors receive weekly or bi-weekly updates, progress milestones, budget tracking summaries, and exit timeline updates.
